Context

In 2020, Covid-19 caused many companies to face an unprecedented challenge in their journey, many of which were unable to survive. The lockdown, necessary for the moment, made many business models unfeasible, especially those not digitized, which overnight were forced to make their digital transformation

At this time, Sebrae in its role as an institution that provides support to entrepreneurs, has become more essential than ever, which has resulted in an increase in its demand for assistance.

This whole situation has caused many companies with digital solutions to start offering their services in an easy or free way during this pandemic period, boosting the migration of offline users to this new world.Analyzing this movement of positive initiatives, we decided to collect b2b solutions with free plans to offer in a single place, a kind of catalog for the entrepreneur.

The process

For this project we formed a squad of 3 members from 2 different units. With the pandemic already underway, all the work was done only with online meetings.

We decided that for the launch we would collect an initial list of solutions, but after the launch, new companies interested in joining the list will be able to submit their solutions through a form that will be on the page.

Strategy and Research

The first steps were to decide the evaluation criteria for a solution to be part of that list. Knowing that there would be great interest from other companies to join the catalog after its launch, our main concern was to create parameters that would ensure the delivery of value to customers without privileging companies during this assessment.

For the launch batch, we hired an innovation consultancy to help us find these solutions. In this first scan, 70 companies were found within the pre-established criteria.

Build it up

With the list in hand, I started the work of building the page. As our portal uses WordPress as a CMS, it took few lines of code to bring the project to life.

The last step before launching was to configure Google Tag Manager to measure the number of accesses in each product. This was boring to solve, since I had never done this before, it took a few days and tips from GTM experts to get it running.

One month was the period between the beginning of the idea and its launch.
